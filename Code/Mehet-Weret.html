Mehet-Weret

Mehet-Weret is thought to have been the goddess of streaming water, she is also credited with creation and rebirth. Her name translates to “Great flood” or “Great Tide”, these names links her with Nu, and it’s waters of chaos. It is said in The Book of the Dead, that Mehet-Weret gave birth to Ra. She is depicted as a woman with the head of a cow, or a cow laying on a mat which is made from reeds. She is normally depicted as wearing the sun disk headdress, in between her horns. Since she is a goddess of water, she is thought to transport pharaohs and gods with her on the water. She is also thought to have brought water to Egypt. She is linked with the nile, and is thought to control the three different versions of the nile. The nile on earth, the nile in underworld and the nile in the sky. (The Egyptians thought that the milky way was another version of the nile).1
