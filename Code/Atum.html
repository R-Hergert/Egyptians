Atum

Atum is one of the oldest gods from ancient Egypt. He is one of the nine main gods from his time period of the Old Kingdom. He is thought to have been one of the creator gods, alongside Ra, Horakhty, and Khepri. His name translates to “the accomplished one”, but it is also thought that his name could mean “the one who did not come to being yet”, which is much more negative than the first translation. It is thought that he gave birth to the genders of the gods, through his semen. Another interpretation says that he gave birth to the entire universe, and everything inside of it. This thought of him is mostly in the Old Kingdom, whereas Ra is the god which comes to prominence later in the Old Kingdom, and in the first Intermediate Period. He is one of the eight-nine most frequently mentioned gods from the Pyramid Texts, which describe him as the god of creation and totality. This entails that he not only creates life and the entire universe, but he can also destroy it. This event is described in the Book of the Dead, where Atum describes to Osiris that he will one day plunge all of the world back into the water of Nu, and that they will be the only survivors, and survive as serpents. Atum is also related to sun in this capacity and deeply connected with the sun theology, but where Ra is related to the rising sun, Atum is related to the setting sun.1
