Wadjet

Wadjet started as a local god from early Egyptian times but became a symbol of Lower Egypt. She appears as a serpent, sometimes with a human head. She is said to have created the first papyrus plant. Her name is also spelled with a papyrus plant and she is a symbol for ancient egypt which is the land of the papyrus plant. Wedjet also plays the role representing justice. Wadjet is a very good example of how Egyptians incorporated local and foreign gods into their own central pantheon.1
