Neith

Neith was the goddess of war, hunting, weaving and wisdom. She is one of the oldest gods on record, because she was worshipped since the predynastic period (around 3000 BCE). There were several queens throughout the first dynasty, who were named after Neith. She was made the patroness of the city of Sais, which was located in the river delta, which was where the nile branched off into several different rivers before joining the Mediterranean Sea. She had a pair of crossed arrows against a leather shield to represent her, as her symbol. She was mostly depicted as wearing the crown of Lower Egypt on her head, but that would sometimes be replaced with a bow case. She was said to have been the mother of the crocodile god, Sebek and at one time she was thought to have been the mother of Ra.1
