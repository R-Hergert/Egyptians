Anuket

Anuket is the goddess of the nile river. She has two very similar titles, “the embracer” and “the Lady of Embracing”. These titles are associated with her because, in one area of the nile there are two tributaries, (which are two small rivers, that feed into a larger body of water), which are said to be her out stretched arms. By the New Kingdom, her name “the embracer” came to symbolize conception, so she also came to represent fertility. The Greeks believed her to be similar to Hestia, because if her avowed virginity, and they also compared her to Artemis, because she was known to be an excellent hunter. She was most prominent in the northern area of Elephantine Island, where the nile river turns towards Egypt. She is depicted as having the body of a woman, with a head of a gazelle, or simply a woman with headdress made of ostrich feathers. She could also be represented by just a gazelle. The start of the annual flooding was marked by a festival which was held in Anuket’s honour. This had people throwing coins, gold and jewelry into the nile in order to pay homage to her and thank her for the bountiful harvest which was achieved that year. This festival also marked the time in which people would eat fish, which was normally considered a taboo in most of Egypt.1
