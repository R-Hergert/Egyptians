Satet

Satet was the goddess of floods and her name translates “She who shoots forth”, possibly referring the annual flooding of the rivers. Her cult originated in the city of Swenet, which is located near the modern city of Aswān. She was also originally thought to have been a goddess of war, hunting and fertility, and she is also thought to have been the mother of Anuket. She had another name which was translated to “She who runs like an arrow”, some people believe that this is referring to the flowing of the rivers. She is depicted as a woman wearing the Upper Egypt crown, with either antelope or gazelle horns. Some other historians believe that this name is referring to her as being a war goddess, and that it meant that she would protect Upper Egypt from the southern border. She would also kill all enemies of the pharaoh. She is always seen holding an ankh, which is an Egyptian cross.1
