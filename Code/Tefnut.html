Tefnut

Tefnut is the goddess of moisture and rain. She is the partner of Shu and the mother of Geb and Nut, the sky and earth gods. 1 Her main symbol is a lioness and often wears the head of a lion but also wears a sun disk on her head. She was often regarded as a spiteful god and her role in the emotional realm was often that of vengeance. Particularly turning sorrow into vengence. 2 One popular myth of creation was that when Tefnut and Shu were born, they went into the world. Shu gave the world life while Tefnut gave the world order. However, their father Amun was still in the chaos and worried over where his children had gone. When they returned, Amun shed tears of joy and when these tears touched the ground, they created men and women. These people still had nowhere to live, so Shu and Tefnut created Geb and Nut, the earth and the sky.
