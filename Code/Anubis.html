Anubis

Anubis was a god of funerals and death. He was portrayed as a man with the head of a jackal. Throughout history, he was the god who was in charge of of funerals and funeral rituals as well as praying for the dead while Osiris was the god who dealt with the dead once they were in the underworld. Because of this, Anubis was said to be the start of many funeral practises such as embalming. This was until the cult of Osiris became popular and Osiris became the dominant god of death.1 He was said to have embalmed Osiris so that Isis could revive him and is often portrayed as following Isis.2 Anubis and Osiris are both important in seeing the importance of funerals in Egypt. He also shows the ever changing importance of individual gods as he Osiris replaced him in the later times.
