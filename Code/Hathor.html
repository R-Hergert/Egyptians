Hathor

Hathor was originally a personification of the milky way, but as time passed she took on many attributes of the other goddesses. She was a goddess of beauty and patron of cosmetic arts. She was seen as the embodiment of joy, love, romance, perfume, dance, music, and alcohol. She took the form of many things, such as a cat, a woman, a goose, and a lion.1
