Maashes

Maashes was the ancient Egyptian lion-god of war. Both a god of war and a guardian and a lord of the horizon. Maashes was usually depicted as a lion-headed man carrying a knife or a sword, Maashes sometimes wore the atef crown or the solar disk and uraeus on his head. Occasionally he was portrayed as a lion devouring a captive. He was believed to help Ra fight against Apep in the solar barque each night, a god who protected the pharaoh while he was in battle. By Greek times, he was attributed as being a god of storms and winds. Maashes was a god who seems to have first appeared in the New Kingdom, and is thought to have been a deity of foreign origin.1
