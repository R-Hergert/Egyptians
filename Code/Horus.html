Horus

Horus was one of the most popular gods in Egypt. He was the god of the sky and pharaohs. He was portrayed as a man with the head of a falcon. While other more important gods (Atum, Aten, Ra, etc) were often more important in their particular time, they were not always recognized by other time periods or areas of Egypt. Horus was an important god who was recognized almost universally in Egypt as well as having many local versions.1 When Set killed Horus’s father Osiris, Horus and Set fought over the throne of Egypt. Horus won the throne and this is often used as a symbol of true Egypt and ruler winning over others. In this battle, Horus’s left eye, which represents the moon, was damaged. This is the explanation for why the moon has cycles.2 Horus shows us how Egyptians looked at the Pharoah’s. He is important when looking at Pharoah’s because he is a strong god who represents pharaohs and is recognized all over egypt, showing a sense of there being a true pharaoh ship that can’t be changed. The fact that he is almost always considered to be a symbol of true pharaohs winning is an important concept.
