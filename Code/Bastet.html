Bastet

Bastet is generally thought of as a cat goddess, but originally had the head of a lion or a desert sand cat. The new kingdom is when she was commonly associated with the domesticated cat. She was worshipped all over lower Egypt. Bastet is often shown holding the ankh and is often accompanied by a litter of kittens. During the old kingdom she was considered to be the daughter of Atum, but she was generally thought to be a daughter of Ra. She was also the wife of Ptah and mother of Nefertum and Maahes.1
