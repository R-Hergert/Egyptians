Khepri

Khepri was the god of the rising sun, often considered an aspect of Ra. His job was to push the sun across the sky and is portrayed as having the head of a scarab beetle or just a scarab beetle. Worship of the scarab beetle far predated Ra and Jewelry and charms based on scarab beetles were very popular. This tradition survived long enough to influence even Roman soldiers.1 Khepri was usually considered to be self-created, but he was sometimes said to be a child of one of the original gods (see the tree of the gods). Even though there is no specific cult or major temple to Khepri, it is thought that there was probably a statue of Khepri in most or all of the Egyptian temples.2 Khepri is one of the ancient ideas that only grew with popularity in Egypt and shows the connection between the natural world (scarab beetles rolling dung) and their mythology (rolling the sun accross the sky).
