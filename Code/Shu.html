Shu

Shu is the god of air. He is the partner of Tefnut and the father of Geb and Nut, the sky and earth gods. 1 He is often considered more lawful that Tefnut, who is often connected to vengeance. 2 One popular myth of creation was that when Tefnut and Shu were born, they went into the world. Shu gave the world life while Tefnut gave the world order. However, their father Amun was still in the chaos and worried over where his children had gone. When they returned, Amun shed tears of joy and when these tears touched the ground, they created men and women. These people still had nowhere to live, so Shu and Tefnut created Geb and Nut, the earth and the sky.
