Apis

Apis bulls was considered to be manifestations of Osiris and Ptah themselves. Only one Apis bull could exist at a time and when the current one died, the Egyptians would go looking for another one and bring it back to Memphis as the new Apis bull. When a new bull was found, it was lead through the streets in celebration for seven days. Even the mother, if known, was highly esteemed and would be called an Isis bull. When an Apis bull died, they would mummify and give a proper burial for the bull.1
