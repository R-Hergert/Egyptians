Set

Set is the god of the desert. He is also often associated with war and chaos.1 He is depicted as some strange animal that most agree is probably made up. This made up animal has been called the Sha but the most common idea of what animal it could have been based on is the pig.2 He was the son of Nut and Geb and his siblings were Osiris, Isis and Nephthys. His wife was Nephthys but he was described as being an unfaithful husband and they never had any children. His story was that he was jealous that his brother Osiris was Pharaoh so he murdered Osiris, cut him into pieces, and cast the pieces all over europe. Osiris’s wife Isis then gave birth to Horus who battles Set to become Pharaoh while Isis went on a quest to revive her husband. Osiris was revived and Horus become Pharaoh but Set continued to be an important god.1 Set was often described as slaying Apep in the nightly journey of Ra and seen as the defender of Ra/ally of Egypt until the cult of Osiris become popular and denounced Set from the pantheon. Set’s name was erased from records and was declared a god of invaders.3
