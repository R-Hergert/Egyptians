Mut

Mut was a sky goddess, who came into prominence during the 18th dynasty. She is represented in hieroglyphs by a lion’s head, or a woman who has a lion’s head. Her names translates to “mother”, she was a considerably older god, in terms of depiction, then other gods. She was associated with Uraeus, the rearing cobra, lions as stated previously, and royal crowns. She was shown wearing two crowns, one from Southern Egypt, and one from Northern Egypt. She was said to have been the companion of Amon and Thebes, together they formed the Theban triad, which was a cult dedicated to these three gods. There is a principle festival which was dedicated to Mut, at the city of Thebes. It was centred around her navigation ability on the horseshoe lake, which surrounded her temple. The god Khons is said to be her son. It is said that the Theban triad conceived him.1
